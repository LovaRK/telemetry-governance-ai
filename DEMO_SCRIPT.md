### KPI Gauges — Row 1

**ROI Score**

> "This is the headline number. It tells you: across all your indexed data, what fraction of it is genuinely earning its license spend?"

Formula:
```
ROI Score = average(composite_score) across all sourcetypes
```
- Range: 0–100
- Higher = more of your data is high-value
- A score below 30 means most of your Splunk data is Low-Value or Wasteful

**GainScope %**

> "GainScope answers the question: what percentage of your daily ingest volume is in the top two value tiers — Critical and Important?"

Formula:
```
GainScope % = (Total GB/day in Tier 1 + Tier 2) / Total GB/day × 100
```
- 5% GainScope means 95% of your daily ingest is Nice-to-Have or Wasteful
- The goal is to push this number up over time — not by ingesting more, but by removing what does not belong

**How each sourcetype is scored (the engine behind these numbers):**

Every sourcetype gets three sub-scores, then a composite:

```
Utilization Score  = (weighted usage activity / max activity in portfolio) × 100
                     — Alerts, scheduled searches, dashboards, ad-hoc queries, users all count
                     — Weights: alerts ×3, scheduled ×3, dashboards ×2, ad-hoc ×1, users ×2
                     — The most-used sourcetype in your environment always scores 100
                     — All others scale proportionally (environment-relative)

Detection Score    = (0.40 × potential detection coverage) + (0.60 × realized detection usage)
                     — Potential: does this sourcetype map to any MITRE ATT&CK techniques?
                     — Realized: are those techniques actually used in active detections?
                     — Pure security-unused data scores low here

Quality Score      = max(0,  100 − (weighted_issue_density × 2000))
                     — Issue density = parser errors / approximate event count
                     — DateParserVerbose errors count ×0.5 (noisy but less harmful)
                     — Other issues count ×1.0
                     — Clean data defaults to 100

Composite Score    = (0.35 × Utilization) + (0.40 × Detection) + (0.25 × Quality)
```

**Configurable weight profiles:**

| Profile | Utilization | Detection | Quality | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Balanced (default) | 35% | 40% | 25% | General use |
| Security First | 25% | 50% | 25% | SOC-focused orgs |
| Operations First | 50% | 25% | 25% | IT Ops-focused orgs |
| Data Quality | 30% | 30% | 40% | Compliance-driven orgs |

### Tier Distribution

> "Once every sourcetype has a composite score, it falls into one of four tiers. Think of it like a report card for your Splunk data."

| Tier | Composite Score | Meaning | Recommended Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Critical** | ≥ 65 | Actively used, security-relevant, high quality | KEEP — never touch |
| **Important** | ≥ 40 | Useful but not mission-critical | KEEP or light optimization |
| **Nice-to-Have** | ≥ 20 | Low usage, limited detection value | OPTIMIZE or ARCHIVE |
| **Low Value / Wasteful** | < 20 | Rarely or never used, no security value | ELIMINATE or ARCHIVE to S3 |

The donut chart shows how your portfolio distributes across these four tiers — both by sourcetype count and by GB/day.

---

### Annual Spend by Tier (Spend Risk Matrix)

> "This chart answers the most important financial question: how much money am I spending on each tier? A large bar on the Low-Value column is money you can recover."

Shows annual license spend broken down by tier. Each bar is calculated as:
```
Tier N Annual Spend = Σ (daily_gb × 365 × $/GB/year) for all sourcetypes in that tier
```

### Quick Wins

> "Quick Wins are Low-Value sourcetypes where the estimated savings are meaningful AND the elimination risk is low. These are the first actions we recommend. One click in this panel submits an approval to the governance workflow."

A Quick Win is flagged when:
- Tier is Wasteful or Low-Value
- Utilization score < 10 (nobody is using it)
- No active detections depend on it
- Estimated savings > threshold

| State | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Pending** | AI recommendation waiting for human review |

### Trust Inspection page

> "This is the engine room. Trust Inspection lets you look up any index by name and see its complete trust state — drift status, confidence score decomposition, reanalysis queue position."

**Trust levels:** `CAUTION` → `RELIABLE` → `TRUSTED`

An index earns trust by:
- Accumulating consistent AI decisions over time (7-day, 14-day, 30-day milestones)
- Having those decisions confirmed by human reviews
- Showing no significant drift between analysis runs

When the AI changes its recommendation for an index, the trust level resets — this is drift detection.

## The Full Flow: How One Analysis Works

> "Let me show you the full pipeline in one sequence:"

```
1. You upload a CSV export from Splunk (or connect live)
                ↓
2. datasensAI ingests the data — index names, sourcetypes, 
   daily volume, utilization signals, detection mappings
                ↓
3. Scoring Engine runs deterministically:
   — Utilization score per sourcetype
   — Detection score per sourcetype
   — Quality score per sourcetype
   — Composite score → Tier assignment
                ↓
4. Recommendation Engine generates candidates:
   — Deterministic rules (not AI) decide KEEP / OPTIMIZE / ELIMINATE / ARCHIVE
   — These rules are auditable and reproducible
                ↓
5. AI Explanation Layer adds human language:
   — LLM receives ONLY the pre-computed, audited numbers
   — LLM generates a narrative explanation (cannot change the recommendation)
   — Every number in the narrative must exist in the grounding data (Gate 2 invariant)
                ↓
6. Certification Gate runs 8 checks:
   — Recommendation stability, grounding integrity, 
     tier consistency, narrative coherence
   — If any gate fails, the decision does not publish
                ↓
7. Governance Workflow opens for human review
                ↓
8. Admin/Operator approves or rejects
                ↓
9. Decision written to immutable audit log
```
